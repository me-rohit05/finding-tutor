HI everyone I need help with a math quetion it is making me crazy: (a , b , c) in Z . a+1/ (b+1/c) = 15/4 => a+b+c =?

You’re given a continued fraction a+1b+1c=154 Such a way of writing a rational number is essentially unique: start with the greatest integer less than or equal to 15/4 , so you have 154=3+34 Take the reciprocal of 3/4 and do the same 43=1+13 Stop here, because the fraction has numerator 1 . Now we have 154=3+43=3+143=3+11+13 and you can conclude that a=3,b=1,c=3

If 1=5, 2=10, 3=17 4=23, then 5=?

It seems like the answer should be 5=1 as the question has mentioned that 1=5 at first. However, 5=1 is actually an incorrect answer. That answer only can be correct if you use the non-mathematical method to solve this trick question. In mathematics, the symbol “= ” in here is actually representing the symbol of function, but not representing the common meaning of “equals to”. Therefore, the question is actually have to be understood like this f(1)=5 f(2)=10 f(3)=17 f(4)=23 Since there are no pattern can be observed in these functions, the answer of f(5) is undefined. I guess the quetioner has mistakenly written the f(2)=10 instead of f(2)=11 . If the f(2)=11 is correct, then the whole series can be observed as f(n)=6n−1 Therefore f(5)=6(5)−1 =29

What are the 3 laws of motion by Sir Isaac Newton?

Sir Isaac Newton’s said that if a body is in motion then it will be in a motion with a constant velocity until an external force is not acting on it. or if a body is in rest (v=0) it will be in rest until an external force is not acting on it. e.g. if a ball is in a motion on a frictionless surface (friction force is ‘0’) and external forces are zero, it will continue the uniform motion. 2nd law of motion, In this law Sir Newton said that the rate of change of momentum of a body is proportional to the external force applied to the body. Force∝Δp/Δt , {where Δp is change in momentum and Δt is change in time }. OR Force = mass × acceleration 3rd law of motion, Whenever one body exerts a force on a second body, the second body exerts an equal and opposite force on first. or simply we can say that for every action their is an equal and opposite reaction. e.g. a swimmer pushes against the water, while the water pushes back on the swimmer.
